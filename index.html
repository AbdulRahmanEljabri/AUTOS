MERCEDES C63S

In the world of high-performance cars, the Mercedes-AMG C63S is one of the best on sale in any class. The AMG badge is synonymous with devastating straight-line paceIt delivers rear-wheel-drive handling that will put a grin on any driver's face, and that applies whether you're piloting the saloon, estate, coupe or even the convertible as they are all powered by the same muscular engine.

The C63S has a number of rivals. The coupe is the sportiest option, and has opponents in the shape of the BMW M4 Coupe, Audi RS 5 Coupe, Lexus RC F, Alpina B4 and even the Nissan GT-R. The saloon takes on the BMW M3 , Alfa Romeo Quadrifoglio, Audi RS5 Sportback and Alpina B3, while the C63 Estate challenges the Audi RS4 Avant and Alpina B3 Touring. The C63 S Cabriolet has rivals in the shape of the BMW M4 Convertible and Alpina B4 Convertible.

BMW M5

You come into contact with the BMW M5, you’re in the company of a legend. The Munich-based firm’s M-for-Motorsport division has been crafting fabled performance versions of BMW’s sporty executive saloon since 1986. The line started with a modified six-cylinder engine from BMW’s M1 supercar grafted into a 535i model, and won instant kudos as the fastest saloon car in production in its day. There have been M5 performance versions of subsequent 5 Series generations right up to the present day, and while enthusiasts have their individual favourites, each version has raised the bar further in terms of performance and luxury.

The BMW M5 is powered by a 4.4-litre V8 engine that features twin turbos and backs up its 592bhp with a mighty 750Nm of torque. With the help of its rapid-fire eight-speed automatic gearbox and four-wheel drive traction the M5 screams to 62mph in the mind-blowingly quick time of 3.4 seconds. Top speed is 155mph, but choose the M Driver package and BMW will override the engine limiter giving you a top speed of 190mph. Selecting the Competition pack barely affects performance, but it does shave a tenth of the 0-62mph time – down to 3.3 seconds.

AUDI RS7

In 2016 Audi introduced the RS 7 Performance. This gave the car an extra 45bhp, while 21-inch alloys, privacy glass, a titanium grey styling pack and sports exhaust helped distinguish it from the standard car. As standard, the RS7 Sportback was a little under £87,000 and cam with a 552bhp 4.0-litre twin-turbo V8.There were 20-inch alloy wheels, LED headlights, MMI Navigation plus, RS Super Sports front seats, a powered tailgate and an electric slide and tilt glass sunroof.

As the RS 7 Sportback is a bit of a unique machine, there aren't really any direct rivals. The closest will be the Porsche Panamera, but while that car will be more driver-focused, the RS 7 is far better looking. You could consider its RS 6 stablemate, ot there's the Mercedes-AMG options of the E 63 and CLS 63.
